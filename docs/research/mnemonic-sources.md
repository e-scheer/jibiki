### Method notes (Tofugu)
- **Dakuten/handakuten get NO individual images** - one consonant-shift
  mini-story per row (k→g car/guard rail, s→z saw zaps, t→d "ta-da!",
  h→b bar/punch; the ° maru = a little fist → p). Voiced kana are variants of
  the base shape, not new drawings.
- Katakana leans on hiragana: ウカキセヘモヤリ taught as "same as your hiragana".
- シ/ツ vs ソ/ン is systemic: horizontal strokes = a FACE (シ, ン), vertical
  strokes = NEEDLES (ツ, ソ); ニ continues the needle theme.
- Built-in contrast pairs: る has the loop / ろ doesn't; ぬ loopy vs ね cat-tail.
- Yōon (きゃ…), small っ and long-vowel ー taught as rules, not mnemonics.
- Practice: small batch → immediate quiz → re-quiz at 5-10 min gaps, always
  remixing older rows in.

## 2. WaniKani - public radical listing (~490 names)

Sources (all public, no login):
- https://www.wanikani.com/radicals (+ ?difficulty=pleasant/painful/death/hell/paradise/reality)
- https://knowledge.wanikani.com/wanikani/japanese/radical-names/
- https://knowledge.wanikani.com/getting-started/wanikani/japanese/radicals-kanji-vocab/

Method (paraphrased): "radical" = any visual component, traditional or invented,
because the Kangxi 214 don't cover every shape and their classical names don't
stick. Each component gets one short concrete English name; kanji stories then
reuse those names verbatim as a cast of characters, encoding meaning + reading.
Naming style: literal shape names (Ground 一, Stick 丨, Fins ハ), real meanings
kept when the component is a kanji (Tree 木, Woman 女), deliberately vivid
inventions for abstract shapes (Narwhal ナ, Tsunami 氵, Viking 龸, Geoduck 頁).
The absurd names cluster on shapes with no obvious pictographic reading.

### Method notes (JF)
- L'ancre est presque toujours la PREMIÈRE syllabe d'un mot L1 du quotidien,
  typographiquement mise en avant ; le glyphe kana est dessiné en noir DANS
  l'illustration plus claire.
- Quelques mnémoniques méta/structurels plutôt que picturaux (た = t+a,
  ん = n cursif, ン = ノ+trait, ケ = le K de OK).
- Paires liées volontaires pour les kana proches (る bague avec rubis /
  ろ la même bague volée) - même astuce que Tofugu る/ろ.
- Les formes apparentées hiragana/katakana réutilisent le même concept
  (う/ウ, も/モ, り/リ, を/ヲ…).

### Le patron commun des trois traditions asiatiques (à voler)
(1) mot/interjection natif dont la PREMIÈRE syllabe ≈ la lecture du kana, (2) objet dessinable où les traits du glyphe s'incrustent littéralement, (3) objets du quotidien chargés culturellement (ojek, jarre โอ่ง, louche vá). Les voyelles = interjections. Fiabilité : kanji123.org (thaï) a produit une extraction FABRIQUÉE, écartée ; fun-japan « kuda » non vérifié.
